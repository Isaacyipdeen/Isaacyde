Sleep Deprivation

Effects of 42 Hr of Total Sleep Deprivation on Component Processes of Verbal Working Memory

Working memory: Maintenance of information in an activated state over brief periods of time (Atkinson & Shiffrin, 1968) Not a one-dimensional construct Baddeley's multicomponent model of working memory: 1) Central Executive: -Direct attention to relevant information; suppresses irrelevant information -Coordination of the slave systems 2) Phonological Loop: -Consists of the articulatory loop and the acoustic store (or phonological store) -Acoustic store with auditory memory traces that are subject to rapid decay -Articulatory loop can revive the memory traces 3) Visuo-spatial sketchpad. Processes visual and spatial information 4) Episodic Buffer -Acts as a buffer store between components of working memory, perception and long-term memory. -Allows individuals to imagine new concepts -Maintains a sense of time
